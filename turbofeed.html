How do I fill it up?

Buy, hire, or rent an auger or seed tender.

Probably the most economical way to fill your machine is to buy a pull-behind auger and a gravity wagon. Of course, if you're just getting started you can find services to do it for you. If you really want the most convenience and the least amount of effort to fill your machine, we recommend buying a seed tender truck. How much does it hold?

We currently have two sizes available for the polymer tops on our machine, 118 Bushel and 150 Bushel. These two sizes give you flexibility on how you want to operate your machine. The 118 Bushel capacity is designed to be filled by a short pull behind auger, or seed tender truck. At just over twelve feet tall, the short top does not require expensive machinery to fill and can even fit under most industrial roofed areas.

The 150 bushel capacity will require a slightly longer auger to fill, but obviously holds more product. We should note that depending on how much product you sell, it's important not to hold too much product at any given time. This is because you want to keep corn fresh and minimize the chances of pests appearing in your product. If corn is sitting in your machine for longer than a month, you are probably over filling it. The maize weevil has a reproductive time of 30 days, so you shouldn't give them enough time to multiply. Luckily, we have sized our machines appropriately to hold enough corn but not too much, and the two sized tops are to cover low volume and high volume markets.
